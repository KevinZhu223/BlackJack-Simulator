Card Counting Guide

What is Card Counting?

Card counting is a strategy used in blackjack to determine whether the next hand is likely to give an advantage to the player or the dealer. By tracking the ratio of high to low cards remaining in the shoe, skilled players can adjust their bets and decisions accordingly.

The Hi-Lo System

The Hi-Lo system is the most popular counting method. Each card is assigned a value:

2, 3, 4, 5, 6
+1
Low cards
7, 8, 9
0
Neutral
10, J, Q, K, A
−1
High cards

Running Count

The Running Count (RC) is the sum of all Hi-Lo values for every card you've seen dealt since the last shuffle. When a 5 is dealt, add +1. When a King is dealt, subtract 1. Keep a running total in your head.

💡 Tip: Practice counting in pairs — a low card (+1) and a high card (−1) cancel out to zero. Train yourself to spot these cancellations quickly.

True Count

The True Count (TC) adjusts for how many decks are left:

True Count = Running Count ÷ Decks Remaining

Example: If the RC is +6 and there are about 3 decks left, the TC is +2. The true count is what really matters for decision-making in multi-deck games.

Using the Count

Positive TC (+2 or higher): The remaining shoe is rich in high cards (10s and Aces). This favors the player — increase your bets!

Negative TC (−2 or lower): The remaining shoe has more low cards. This favors the dealer — bet the minimum.

Neutral (−1 to +1): No significant edge — play basic strategy with standard bets.

Practice Tips

🎯 Start by memorizing the Hi-Lo values until they're automatic — you shouldn't have to think about whether a 5 is +1.
⚡ Count cards in pairs as they're dealt. Two cancelling cards = skip them mentally.
🔄 Use the "Check My Count" quiz after each hand to test your accuracy.
👀 Use "Peek at Count" sparingly — try to calculate it yourself first!
📈 Aim for 90%+ accuracy before you try adjusting your bets based on the count.
